17    /**
18    * This class implements a <i>Merton Model</i>, that is, it provides the drift and volatility specification
19    * and performs the calculation of the numeraire (consistent with the dynamics, i.e. the drift).
20    *
21    * The model is
22    * \[
23    * dS = \mu S dt + \sigma S dW + S dJ, \quad S(0) = S_{0},
24    * \]
25    * \[
26    * dN = r N dt, \quad N(0) = N_{0},
27    * \]
28    * where \( W \) is Brownian motion and \( J \) is a jump process (compound Poisson process).
29    *
30    * The process \( J \) is given by \( J(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} (Y_{i}-1) \), where
31    * \( \log(Y_{i}) \) are i.i.d. normals with mean \( a - \frac{1}{2} b^{2} \) and standard deviation \( b \).
32    * Here \( a \) is the jump size mean and \( b \) is the jump size std. dev.
33    *
34    * The model can be rewritten as \( S = \exp(X) \), where
35    * \[
36    * dX = \mu dt + \sigma dW + dJ^{X}, \quad X(0) = \log(S_{0}),
37    * \]
38    * with
39    * \[
40    * J^{X}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} \log(Y_{i})
41    * \]
42    * with \( \mu = r - \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 - (exp(a)-1) \lambda \).
43    *
44    * The class provides the model of S to an <code>{@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess}</code> via the specification of
45    * \( f = exp \), \( \mu = r - \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 - (exp(a)-1) \lambda \), \( \lambda_{1,1} = \sigma, \lambda_{1,2} = a - \frac{1}{2} b^2, \lambda_{1,3} = b \), i.e.,
46    * of the SDE
47    * \[
48    * dX = \mu dt + \lambda_{1,1} dW + \lambda_{1,2} dN + \lambda_{1,3} Z dN, \quad X(0) = \log(S_{0}),
49    * \]
50    * with \( S = f(X) \). See {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess} for the notation.
51    *
52    * For an example on the construction of the three factors \( dW \), \( dN \), and \( Z dN \) see {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.assetderivativevaluation.MonteCarloMertonModel}.
53    *
54    * @author Christian Fries
55    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.assetderivativevaluation.MonteCarloMertonModel
56    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess The interface for numerical schemes.
57    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel The interface for models provinding parameters to numerical schemes.
58    * @version 1.0
59    */
