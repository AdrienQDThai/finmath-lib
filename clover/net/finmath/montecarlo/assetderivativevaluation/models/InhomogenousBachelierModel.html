13    /**
14    * This class implements a (variant of the) Bachelier model, that is, it provides the drift and volatility specification
15    * and performs the calculation of the numeraire (consistent with the dynamics, i.e. the drift).
16    *
17    * The model is
18    * \[
19    * dS = r S dt + \sigma dW, \quad S(0) = S_{0},
20    * \]
21    * \[
22    * dN = r N dt, \quad N(0) = N_{0},
23    * \]
24    *
25    * The class provides the model of S to an <code>{@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess}</code> via the specification of
26    * \( f = \text{identity} \), \( \mu = \frac{exp(r \Delta t_{i}) - 1}{\Delta t_{i}} S(t_{i}) \), \( \lambda_{1,1} = \sigma \frac{exp(-2 r t_{i}) - exp(-2 r t_{i+1})}{2 r \Delta t_{i}} \), i.e.,
27    * of the SDE
28    * \[
29    * dX = \mu dt + \lambda_{1,1} dW, \quad X(0) = \log(S_{0}),
30    * \]
31    * with \( S = X \). See {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess} for the notation.
32    *
33    * The model's implied Bachelier volatility for a given maturity T is
34    * <code>volatility * Math.sqrt((Math.exp(2 * riskFreeRate * optionMaturity) - 1)/(2*riskFreeRate*optionMaturity))</code>
35    *
36    * @author Christian Fries
37    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess The interface for numerical schemes.
38    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel The interface for models provinding parameters to numerical schemes.
39    * @version 1.0
40    */
