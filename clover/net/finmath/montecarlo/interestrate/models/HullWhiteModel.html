38    /**
39    * Implements a Hull-White model with time dependent mean reversion speed and time dependent short rate volatility.
40    *
41    * <p>
42    * <b>Model Dynamics</b>
43    * </p>
44    *
45    * The Hull-While model assumes the following dynamic for the short rate:
46    * \[ d r(t) = ( \theta(t) - a(t) r(t) ) d t + \sigma(t) d W(t) \text{,} \quad r(t_{0}) = r_{0} \text{,} \]
47    * where the function \( \theta \) determines the calibration to the initial forward curve,
48    * \( a \) is the mean reversion and \( \sigma \) is the instantaneous volatility.
49    *
50    * The dynamic above is under the equivalent martingale measure corresponding to the numeraire
51    * \[ N(t) = \exp\left( \int_0^t r(\tau) \mathrm{d}\tau \right) \text{.} \]
52    *
53    * The main task of this class is to provide the risk-neutral drift and the volatility to the numerical scheme (given the volatility model), simulating
54    * \( r(t_{i}) \). The class then also provides and the corresponding numeraire and forward rates (LIBORs).
55    *
56    * <p>
57    * <b>Time Discrete Model</b>
58    * </p>
59    *
60    * Assuming piecewise constant coefficients (mean reversion speed \( a \) and short
61    * rate volatility \( \sigma \) the class specifies the drift and factor loadings as
62    * piecewise constant functions for an Euler-scheme.
63    * The class provides the exact Euler step for the joint distribution of
64    * \( (r,N) \), where \( r \) denotes the short rate and \( N \) denotes the
65    * numeraire, following the scheme in <a href="http://ssrn.com/abstract=2737091">ssrn.com/abstract=2737091</a>.
66    *
67    * More specifically (assuming a constant mean reversion speed \( a \) for a moment), considering
68    * \[ \Delta \bar{r}(t_{i}) = \frac{1}{t_{i+1}-t_{i}} \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} d r(t) \]
69    * we find from
70    * \[ \exp(-a t) \ \left( \mathrm{d} \left( \exp(a t) r(t) \right) \right) \ = \ a r(t) + \mathrm{d} r(t) \ = \ \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \]
71    * that
72    * \[ \exp(a t_{i+1}) r(t_{i+1}) - \exp(a t_{i}) r(t_{i}) \ = \ \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(a t) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(a t) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
73    * that is
74    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ -(1-\exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t_{i})) r(t_{i}) + \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
75    *
76    * Assuming piecewise constant \( \sigma \) and \( \theta \), being constant over \( (t_{i},t_{i}+\Delta t_{i}) \), we thus find
77    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} \left( ( \theta(t_{i}) - a \bar{r}(t_{i})) \Delta t_{i} \right) + \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \Delta W(t_{i}) \] .
78    *
79    * In other words, the Euler scheme is exact if the mean reversion \( a \) is replaced by the effective mean reversion
80    * \( \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} a \) and the volatility is replaced by the
81    * effective volatility \( \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \).
82    *
83    * In the calculations above the mean reversion speed is treated as a constants, but it is straight
84    * forward to see that the same holds for piecewise constant mean reversion speeds, replacing
85    * the expression \( a \ t \) by \( \int_{0}^t a(s) \mathrm{d}s \).
86    *
87    * <p>
88    * <b>Calibration</b>
89    * </p>
90    *
91    * The drift of the short rate is calibrated to the given forward curve using
92    * \[ \theta(t) = \frac{\partial}{\partial T} f(0,t) + a(t) f(0,t) + \phi(t) \text{,} \]
93    * where the function \( f \) denotes the instantanenous forward rate and
94    * \( \phi(t) = \frac{1}{2} a \sigma^{2}(t) B(t)^{2} + \sigma^{2}(t) B(t) \frac{\partial}{\partial t} B(t) \) with \( B(t) = \frac{1-\exp(-a t)}{a} \).
95    *
96    * <p>
97    * <b>Volatility Model</b>
98    * </p>
99    *
100    * The Hull-White model is essentially equivalent to LIBOR Market Model where the forward rate <b>normal</b> volatility \( \sigma(t,T) \) is
101    * given by
102    * \[ \sigma(t,T_{i}) \ = \ (1 + L_{i}(t) (T_{i+1}-T_{i})) \sigma(t) \exp(-a (T_{i}-t)) \frac{1-\exp(-a (T_{i+1}-T_{i}))}{a (T_{i+1}-T_{i})} \]
103    * (where \( \{ T_{i} \} \) is the forward rates tenor time discretization (note that this is the <b>normal</b> volatility, not the <b>log-normal</b> volatility)
104    * (see <a href="http://ssrn.com/abstract=2737091">ssrn.com/abstract=2737091</a> for details on the derivation).
105    * Hence, we interpret both, short rate mean reversion speed and short rate volatility as part of the <i>volatility model</i>.
106    *
107    * The mean reversion speed and the short rate volatility have to be provided to this class via an object implementing
108    * {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel}.
109    *
110    * This implementation supports different method for the interpolation of the curves.
111    * The property <code>"isInterpolateDiscountFactorsOnLiborPeriodDiscretization"</code> is a boolean. If true, the
112    * given curves are used only at the discretization points given by <code>liborPeriodDiscretization</code>.
113    * This implies that the model reports only a limited set of risk factors in the methods {@link HullWhiteModel#getModelParameters()}.
114    *
115    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel
116    * @see <a href="http://ssrn.com/abstract=2737091">ssrn.com/abstract=2737091</a>
117    *
118    * @author Christian Fries
119    * @version 1.4
120    */
